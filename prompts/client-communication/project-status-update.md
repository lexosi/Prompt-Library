# Project Status Update

**Category:** client-communication
**When to use:** Sending a weekly or milestone status update to a client — keeping them informed without requiring a meeting.

---

Write a concise project status update email for a client. The email must give them a clear picture of where things stand without requiring a reply or a meeting to clarify.

Structure it as:
- **Status:** One word or phrase — On Track / At Risk / Delayed — with one sentence of context.
- **Completed this period:** Bullet list of what was done. Focus on outcomes, not activities.
- **In progress:** What's being worked on right now. One bullet per item, with expected completion.
- **Blocked / Needs decision:** Anything waiting on the client or a third party. Be specific — what exactly is needed and by when?
- **Next milestone:** What you're aiming for and when.

Keep the total email under 200 words. Clients should be able to process it in under 60 seconds.

**Context:**
- Project: [name]
- Period covered: [e.g., Week 3, April 14–18]
- Status: [on track / at risk / delayed]
- Completed: [list]
- In progress: [list]
- Blocked: [anything]
- Next milestone: [what, when]

---

## Example output

Subject: [Project Name] — Week 3 Update

**Status: On Track**
We're on schedule for the May 1 delivery.

**Completed (Apr 14–18):**
- AI response pipeline integrated with your product database
- Draft of the onboarding flow — ready for your review (link below)
- Load testing completed: system handles 500 concurrent users without degradation

**In Progress:**
- Admin dashboard — on track for Apr 25
- Final round of QA testing — starts Apr 23

**Needs Decision:**
- Email notification copy: we need your approved copy by Apr 22 to stay on schedule. [Link to draft]

**Next milestone:** Full QA sign-off, Apr 28.

Let me know if you'd like to review the onboarding flow together — otherwise I'll assume the linked version is approved by April 21.

[Name]
