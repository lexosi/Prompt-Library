# AI Product Pitch

**Category:** pitch-and-positioning
**When to use:** Pitching an AI-powered product to investors, early adopters, or enterprise buyers — needs to convey credibility and excitement without hype.

---

Write a compelling pitch for an AI product. The pitch must work in 90 seconds spoken OR as a 250-word written summary. It should make the listener want to see a demo or ask "how does it work?"

Structure:
1. **The hook:** Open with the problem — make the listener feel it, not just understand it.
2. **The insight:** The non-obvious observation that makes your solution possible now, when it wasn't before.
3. **The solution:** What the product does — one concrete, specific sentence.
4. **The proof:** One real number, customer result, or tangible demonstration of traction.
5. **The market:** Who buys this, why they buy it today, and why they can't solve it themselves.
6. **The ask:** What you want from this specific listener — be specific.

Avoid: buzzwords ("leverage AI to transform"), passive construction, vague claims.

**Product:**
[name and one-line description]
**Target buyer:**
[who pays for it]
**Core value:**
[what they get that they couldn't get before]
**Traction (if any):**
[users, revenue, pilots, waitlist]
**Ask:**
[what you want from the pitch]

## Example output

Every mid-sized engineering team has the same silent problem: code review is the bottleneck nobody talks about. PRs sit for 48 hours. Bugs that should have been caught in review reach production. And your best engineers spend 30% of their time reviewing, not building.

We built CodeLens because we realized something: the patterns that make a pull request risky — unchecked inputs, missing auth, N+1 queries — are predictable. They've been documented in thousands of post-mortems. A model trained on that knowledge can catch them in seconds.

CodeLens reviews every PR automatically. It flags security issues, type safety violations, and architectural drift before a human reviewer even opens the diff. Your engineers review the AI's findings, not the raw code. Review time drops from 48 hours to 4 hours.

In our 3-month beta with 8 teams, CodeLens caught 47 issues that reached production review — 12 of which were security-related. One team shipped zero post-merge security incidents in their first month.

We're targeting engineering teams of 10–50 people at SaaS companies. They can't afford a dedicated security reviewer, but they can afford $800/month to not get breached.

We're raising a $1.5M pre-seed round. We're looking for investors who have portfolio companies in the dev tools space — we want warm introductions to CTOs, not just capital.
