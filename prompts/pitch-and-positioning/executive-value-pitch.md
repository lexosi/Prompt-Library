# Executive Value Pitch

**Category:** pitch-and-positioning
**When to use:** Explaining the ROI of AI engineering investment to a non-technical executive — CEO, CFO, or board member.

---

Write a business case for an AI engineering investment addressed to a non-technical executive. This person does not care about the technology — they care about cost, risk, speed, and competitive position. Translate technical work into business terms.

The document must cover:
1. **The business problem:** What is the company losing, spending, or missing today — in dollars, hours, or market position.
2. **The proposed investment:** What is being built and what it costs — without technical jargon.
3. **The expected return:** Specific, measurable outcomes. Time saved × hourly cost = dollars. Errors avoided × average cost per error = dollars. Be conservative.
4. **The timeline to value:** When does the executive start seeing the return? Not "after launch" — a specific date.
5. **The risk of not acting:** What happens to the business if you don't invest? What are competitors doing?
6. **The ask:** A specific decision — budget approval, headcount, timeline commitment.

No bullet-point lists of AI features. No technical architecture. Just business outcomes.

**Investment:**
[what you're proposing to build]
**Cost:**
[total investment]
**Business problem it solves:**
[in business terms]
**Expected outcomes:**
[specific measurable results]

---

## Example output

**The problem:** Our support team handles 800 tickets per week. 65% of those tickets — roughly 520 per week — are the same 35 questions, answered manually by a team that costs $180,000/year in salaries. We're paying six-figure salaries to copy-paste answers.

**The investment:** An AI assistant that answers those 520 weekly questions automatically, without human involvement. Total cost: $40,000 to build, $800/month to operate.

**The return:** If the AI handles 70% of tier-1 tickets (a conservative estimate based on similar deployments), we free up 2 full-time support agents. That's $80,000/year in recaptured capacity. At current growth, we would have hired 2 additional agents by Q4 — this avoids that $80,000/year in new headcount. ROI positive within 7 months.

**Timeline to value:** Live in 6 weeks. Measurable ticket deflection in week 7.

**Risk of not acting:** Our two largest competitors deployed similar systems in Q1. Customer expectations for response time are shifting — 24/7 instant answers are becoming the baseline, not the differentiator. Every month we delay, we make it harder to retain customers who've experienced faster service elsewhere.

**The ask:** $40,000 budget approval and a 6-week engineering timeline starting May 1.
