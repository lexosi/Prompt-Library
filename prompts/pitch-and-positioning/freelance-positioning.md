# Freelance Positioning

**Category:** pitch-and-positioning
**When to use:** Writing your positioning statement as an AI engineering freelancer or consultant — for your website, LinkedIn, or a cold intro.

---

Write a positioning statement for an AI engineering freelancer or consultant. This statement must communicate specifically who you help, what you help them do, and why you're the right person — without sounding like a generic "I help companies leverage AI."

The statement must include:
1. **Who you help:** Specific type of client — size, stage, industry, or role.
2. **What problem you solve:** The specific, felt pain — not "AI strategy" but "your AI features ship but don't perform."
3. **How you solve it:** Your specific method, stack, or approach that differentiates you.
4. **Why you:** The specific experience, track record, or perspective that makes you credible.
5. **The proof:** One concrete result — number, project, or outcome.

Write three versions:
- **Long version (150 words):** For your website "About" or LinkedIn summary.
- **Short version (50 words):** For a cold email intro or Twitter/X bio.
- **One-liner (15 words):** For a conference badge or casual intro.

**Your background:**
[describe your experience, stack, and the types of clients you want]
**Best result you've achieved for a client:**
[specific outcome]
**What you do differently from other AI engineers:**
[your differentiator]

---

## Example output

**Long version:**
I'm an AI Engineering Lead who helps early-stage startups and product teams turn rough AI prototypes into production systems that actually work. Most AI features fail not because the model is wrong, but because the plumbing around it — the prompts, the retrieval, the agent logic, the error handling — wasn't built for production. I specialize in Rust and Python backends, LLM agent orchestration, and RAG pipelines. I've shipped AI features for 6 startups in the last 2 years. My last engagement took a customer support agent from 40% accuracy to 87% in 6 weeks by rebuilding the retrieval layer and redesigning the tool definitions.

**Short version:**
AI Engineering Lead specializing in turning broken AI prototypes into production systems. Rust, Python, LLM agents, RAG. Helped 6 startups ship in the last 2 years. Reach out if your AI feature works in demos but fails in production.

**One-liner:**
I make AI features that work in demos actually work in production.
