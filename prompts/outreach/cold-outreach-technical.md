# Cold Outreach: Technical Leader

**Category:** outreach
**When to use:** Cold message to a technical leader (CTO, VP Engineering, senior engineer) at a company you want to work with, partner with, or sell to.

---

Write a cold outreach message to a technical leader. The message must be specific enough to prove I've done research, short enough to be read in 30 seconds, and end with a frictionless ask.

Rules:
- Under 120 words
- Reference something specific about their work — a blog post, open-source project, job posting, or company announcement
- One clear ask — not "let me know if you want to chat," but a specific, low-commitment question or request
- No pitch for my services in the first message — the goal is a response, not a sale
- No subject line manipulation ("Quick question" / "Following up on my last email" in a first message)

**Context:**
- Who I'm reaching out to: [name, title, company]
- Why specifically them: [what I read/saw that made me reach out]
- My relevant background: [one sentence]
- What I want from them: [specific ask — intro, advice, call, collaboration]
- Channel: [LinkedIn / Email]

---

## Example output

Subject: Your post on Rust async runtimes

Hi [Name],

Your post on building a custom async runtime in Rust without Tokio was the clearest explanation of waker semantics I've read — I've shared it with two of my engineers.

I'm an AI engineering lead who's been building similar low-level infrastructure for LLM serving systems. I ran into the exact backpressure problem you described in section 3, and I solved it differently — I'd be curious whether you explored that path.

No agenda beyond comparing notes. Would you be open to a 20-minute technical conversation?

[Your name]
