# Recruiter Response

**Category:** outreach
**When to use:** Responding to a recruiter message — whether to decline, explore further, or set expectations clearly.

---

Write a response to a recruiter message. The response must be professional, efficient, and honest — it should close the loop clearly without burning a bridge.

Choose the appropriate version:

**OPTION A — Not interested but keep the door open:**
Thank them, decline this specific role, give one honest sentence about why it's not the right fit right now, and ask to be considered for [specific type of role] in the future. Under 80 words.

**OPTION B — Interested but need more info before deciding:**
Express genuine interest, ask the 3 specific questions you need answered before deciding whether to proceed: [compensation range, remote policy, what the AI/engineering stack actually is]. Under 100 words.

**OPTION C — Interested and want to move forward:**
Confirm interest, provide the 2–3 things they need to move forward (availability, resume, LinkedIn), and propose a specific time slot. Under 80 words.

**Context:**
- What the recruiter offered: [role, company]
- Your current situation: [open/not open to opportunities]
- What matters to you in a next role: [1–3 things]
- Which option applies: [A/B/C]

---

## Example output

**Option B — Interested, need more info:**

Hi [Recruiter],

Thanks for reaching out — [Company] is doing genuinely interesting work in the AI infra space and this caught my attention.

Before I commit time to the process, three quick questions:
1. What's the compensation range for this role?
2. Is this remote, hybrid, or in-office? If hybrid, which city and how many days?
3. What does the current AI/ML stack look like — are you building on top of foundation models or training your own?

If those answers are a fit, I'm happy to schedule a first call.

[Your name]
