# AI Thought Leadership Article

**Category:** content-strategy
**When to use:** Writing a thought leadership article on AI engineering that builds credibility and an audience — not a summary of trends but a specific, defensible point of view.

---

You are a content strategist and editor specializing in technical thought leadership. Help me write a thought leadership article on AI engineering.

The article must have:
1. **A specific, debatable claim:** Not "AI agents are changing software development" (obvious) but "AI agents will make senior engineers more valuable, not less, because the bottleneck will shift from code production to judgment calls." The claim must be something a reasonable person could disagree with.
2. **Evidence from experience:** Specific examples, real numbers, or concrete observations — not abstract claims.
3. **A counterargument addressed:** What's the strongest objection to your claim? Address it honestly and directly — this builds credibility.
4. **A practical implication:** What should the reader actually do differently based on this insight?
5. **A memorable closing frame:** The article should end with a reframe, not a summary.

Format: 600–900 words. No section headers — this is an article, not a listicle.

**My claim/point of view:**
[state your specific, debatable position]

**Evidence I have:**
[list the experiences, observations, or data points you can draw from]

**Target reader:**
[who reads this — e.g., senior engineers, AI product managers, tech founders]

**Publication:**
[where this will be published — LinkedIn, personal blog, Substack, Medium]

---

## Example output

**Opening (hook):**
Six months ago I gave a junior engineer on my team a task that would have taken them three days. They finished it in four hours, using Claude as a coding partner. My first reaction was satisfaction. My second was: what does this mean for their career?

[Article continues developing the claim that the shift to AI-augmented development creates a new split: engineers who can judge AI output vs. engineers who just accept it. The former become more valuable; the latter become interchangeable. The article closes with the reframe: "The question isn't whether AI will replace engineers. It's whether you're developing the judgment that AI can't develop for you."]

**Structure used:**
- Para 1: Concrete anecdote that makes the reader feel the tension
- Para 2–3: The claim developed with specific evidence
- Para 4: The strongest counterargument, taken seriously
- Para 5: Why the counterargument doesn't hold
- Para 6: The practical implication — what to do
- Para 7: The reframe (not a summary — a shift in perspective)
