# Technical Blog Strategy

**Category:** content-strategy
**When to use:** Planning a technical blog series that builds a consistent audience over 3–6 months — not one-off posts, but a coherent content system.

---

You are a content strategist specializing in developer audiences. Help me plan a technical blog series that builds a consistent readership. This is not about writing a single post — it's about designing a content system that grows over time.

Design:
1. **Audience definition:** Who reads this? What do they already know? What do they want to know? What do they do after reading?
2. **Content pillars:** 3 recurring themes that the series covers. Each post fits under one pillar. Pillars should be narrow enough to be coherent but broad enough to sustain 12+ posts.
3. **Post formats:** Which 2–3 formats will you use? (Tutorial, opinion/perspective, case study, tool review, postmortem, interview.) Match formats to pillar content.
4. **12-post editorial calendar:** A specific post idea for each of the first 12 posts. Include: working title, pillar, format, one-sentence pitch. Sequence them for maximum momentum — don't put your best post first.
5. **Distribution strategy:** How does each post reach its audience? Beyond "post on LinkedIn" — which communities, newsletters, platforms, and timing.
6. **Growth mechanism:** What makes a reader come back for post 2, 3, and 12? What's the promise you make and keep?

## Example output

**Content pillars:**
1. **AI Systems in Production** — What actually goes wrong when you ship AI features, and how to prevent it. (Case studies, postmortems, tutorials)
2. **UEFN & Game Dev Engineering** — Technical deep-dives into building games with UEFN and Verse. (Tutorials, tool reviews)
3. **Engineering Leadership** — Leading AI agents and human developers — the operational reality. (Opinion, frameworks)

**First 4 posts (momentum sequence):**
1. "The UEFN Verse bug that cost me 3 days (and how to avoid it)" — Pillar 2, Tutorial. Start with a concrete, specific win to establish "this person solves real problems."
2. "Why your AI features work in demos but fail in production" — Pillar 1, Opinion. High-share potential — addresses a universal frustration.
3. "How I structure task delegation to AI agents" — Pillar 3, Framework. Shows leadership expertise.
4. "Building a Roblox economy from scratch: what I got wrong" — Pillar 2, Case study. Personal and specific.

**Growth mechanism:** Each post ends with one actionable takeaway + one question for readers. Reply conversations in comments → new post ideas. Readers come back because every post is immediately useful, not inspirational.
